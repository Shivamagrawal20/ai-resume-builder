Short, practical ideas you can use while you build your resume, run ATS checks, and move through interviews. Pick one tip per week and apply it consistently.

Resume writing

Make every line earn its place—clear outcomes beat buzzwords.

Impact🎯
Lead with action verbs

Open bullets with verbs like “shipped,” “reduced,” or “led.” Pair them with what changed for the team or customer.

Credibility📊
Quantify whenever you can

Percentages, timelines, revenue, and user counts make achievements scannable for recruiters and ATS parsers.

Focus✂️
One page for early career

Unless you have many years of relevant roles, keep a tight one-page story. Cut older or unrelated lines first.

Polish✅
Proofread out loud

Read once for typos, once for tense consistency, and fix formatting glitches that break PDF exports.

ATS & keywords

Help parsers read your file and match the role you want.

Matching🔑
Mirror the job description

Weave in skills and phrases from the posting—naturally, in context—so keyword checks reflect real experience.

Format📄
Keep layout parser-friendly

Single-column layouts, standard headings (Experience, Education), and common fonts reduce parsing errors.

Tailoring🧩
Tweak per application

Adjust your summary and top bullets for each role. A small customization often beats one generic resume for everything.

Check📈
Run ATS before you submit

Use the checker here with the job description to spot missing keywords and weak sections early.

Interviews

Turn preparation into calm, specific answers.

Structure⭐
Use STAR for stories

Situation, Task, Action, Result—keeps behavioral answers short and proves impact without rambling.

Research🔎
Know the company’s angle

Skim recent news, product changes, and values. Tie one concrete detail to why you want this team.

Closing💬
Ask two sharp questions

Ask about success in the role and how the team collaborates—questions you genuinely care about.

Follow-up✉️
Thank-you within 24 hours

Brief note: thanks, one thing you enjoyed discussing, and enthusiasm. No walls of text.

Job search

Stay organized and visible without burning out.

Pipeline📋
Track applications

Spreadsheet or tool: company, role, date sent, status, and link. Makes follow-ups and prep easier.

Profile🌐
Align your headline

Your LinkedIn or portfolio headline should match the role family you’re targeting—not only your current title.

Network🤝
Warm intros beat cold spam

Short, specific asks to people you know—or thoughtful comments on posts—beat generic connection blasts.

Negotiation💼
Research comp ranges first

Know market data for level and location before you talk numbers. Anchor with a range, not a guess.

Ongoing growth

Small habits that compound between applications.

Skills🛠️
Show learning on the resume

Relevant courses, certifications, or side projects belong in a compact section if they support your target role.

Feedback🔄
Iterate after rejections

If you get a pattern of no responses, refresh keywords, summary, or first bullet under each role.
